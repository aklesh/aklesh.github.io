Field & Space Courses

Teaching the next generation of explorers through hands-on engineering in extreme environments and practical space systems design.

Caltech EE 154: Practical Electronics for Space Applications

Subsystem Design and Interface

An intensive hands-on course focused on the practical aspects of electronics design for space environments. Students proceed through a complete design lifecycle‚Äîfrom understanding the harsh space environment (vacuum, radiation, thermal) to designing, building, and testing flight-ready electronic subsystems. The course culminates in a "flight" of prototype subsystems on a high-altitude balloon to near-space conditions, providing real-world validation of student designs.

Macgyver Engineering / Field Engineering

Often referred to as "Macgyver Engineering," this field engineering course challenges students to solve complex technical problems in remote, resource-constrained environments with limited tools. The curriculum emphasizes rapid prototyping, adaptability, and systems thinking in the wild.

This course concept has been taught across multiple institutions including:

  • Arizona State University (Field Engineering)
  • Navajo Technical University
  • Western Michigan University (CubeSat Bootcamp)

Operations for Interplanetary Small Spacecraft

A specialized course covering the unique challenges of operating small spacecraft beyond Low Earth Orbit. Topics include deep space navigation, communication architectures, autonomy, and fault protection for CubeSats and SmallSats venturing to the Moon, Mars, and beyond.

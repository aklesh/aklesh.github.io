Deep Ocean Expeditions

Exploring the 70% of our planet covered by water requires specialized vessels and submersibles capable of withstanding crushing pressures. These expeditions advance our understanding of Earth’s biology and geology while providing direct analogs for future missions to Ocean Worlds.

Expeditions

RV Polarstern (2023)

Objective: Arctic Ocean exploration aboard the legendary German icebreaker Polarstern. * Mission: Investigating the biological connectivity of the Gakkel Ridge. * Role: Served as a scientist/engineer investigating under-ice robotics and oceanography.

HOV Alvin (2022)

Objective: Science verification cruise for the newly upgraded human-occupied submersible Alvin (WHOI). * The Dive: Participated in a dive to 6,500 meters (certification depth) to explore deep-sea seeps and vents. This marked a historic milestone for the vehicle, extending its reach to 99% of the ocean floor. * Science: Documented unexpected biodiversity at extreme depths.

RV Kronprins Haakon (2019/2021)

Objective: HACON cruises to the Arctic. * Focus: Exploring hydrothermal vents at the Gakkel Ridge. * Technology: Deployed the Aurora ROV and other autonomous assets to map and sample these alien-like ecosystems.

WHOI Deep Sea Sediment Sampler

Development: Working with WHOI to develop advanced autonomous samplers capable of retrieving pristine sediment cores from the hadal zone. These samples are critical for understanding the carbon cycle and the history of Earth’s oceans.

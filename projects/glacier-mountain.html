Glacier & Mountain Travel

Exploration often requires venturing into terrain where machines cannot yet go. These expeditions blend technical mountaineering with scientific inquiry, pushing the boundaries of human presence in the cryosphere.

Expeditions

NOLS Denali (2019)

A major expedition to Denali (20,310 ft), the highest peak in North America. * Objective: Leadership training and technical ascent via the West Buttress. * Conditions: Heavy glaciation, extreme cold, and altitude. This expedition focused on team dynamics in high-consequence environments—skills directly transferable to managing spacecraft operations teams.

Matanuska “Robot Spelunker” (2017)

A JPL expedition to the Matanuska Glacier in Alaska to test concepts for exploring the subsurface oceans of Europa. * The Mission: To map the interior of a glacier by exploring “moulins”—vertical shafts carved by meltwater. * The Tech: The team deployed a prototype “spelunking” probe into the icy abyss. Lowered into the moulin, the robot used LIDAR and sonar to map the geometry of the plumbing system, providing data on how water lubricates the glacier’s flow. * Outcome: Successfully mapped the upper chambers of a major moulin system, validating the sensor suite for future off-world applications.

NOLS Alaska Mountaineering (2016)

An intensive training course in the Wrangle-St. Elias Range. * Focus: Glacier travel, crevasse rescue, and snow camping. * Significance: Laid the technical foundation for subsequent scientific deployments on the Matanuska and Taku glaciers.

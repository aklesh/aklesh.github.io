Geology & Volcanology

Understanding the dynamic processes that shape Earth is fundamental to understanding planetary evolution across the solar system.

Kilauea Volcano

2018 Eruption Response: During the historic Lower East Rift Zone eruption, I deployed near the active fissures to support rapid-response monitoring efforts. * Thesis Work: My research focused on the geophysics of the Kilauea plumbing system. * DAS Activity: Worked with Zhongwen Zhan (Caltech) to deploy Distributed Acoustic Sensing arrays, turning fiber optic cables into seismometers to track magma movement in real-time.

Iceland

Volcanology Field School (2022): Investigating the unique divergent plate boundary geology of Iceland. * Volcano Response: Deployed rapid-response instruments to monitor the Fagradalsfjall eruptions, utilizing DAS to detect subtle tremors synonymous with propagating dikes.

Hydrothermal Systems

NUI & Hydrothermal Vents: Using the Nereid Under-Ice (NUI) vehicle to explore chemotrophic ecosystems. These vents are prime analogs for the seafloor of Europa.

Alvin Caribbean Seeps (2022): A deep-dive expedition to the Caribbean trenches to sample cold seeps, where methane-rich fluids support complex life forms in the absence of sunlight.

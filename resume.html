PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE

Jet Propulsion Laboratory (2011-Present)

  • Project Systems Engineer – Lunar Trailblazer (2020-Present) – Technical lead and NASA Engineering Technical Authority for ~200kg lunar orbiting spacecraft. Mission will map water ice on moon using push-broom spectrometer and thermal mapper. Coordinating multiple institutions for spacecraft integration and mission readiness including international partners. Cost constrained within NASA’s SIMPLEX program.
  • Chief Engineer – MarCO (Mars Cube One) Nanospacecraft (2014-2020) – Technical lead for dual 6U CubeSat mission to Mars. Spacecraft were the first nanospacecraft to independently leave Earth’s orbit and fly by Mars. CubeSats launched with InSight, separated, cruised (including 5 trajectory correction maneuvers), and reached Mars in Nov 2018. Craft received InSight EDL UHF data, and re-transmited to the DSN’s 70m dish on Earth. Project was cost constrained, and had limited schedule (concept to flight in 15 months). Additionally, Cognizant Engineer or Contract Technical Manager of several subsystems (Electrical, CDH, EPS, GDS, and Cameras).
  • Engineering Lead – Buoyant Rover for Under-Ice Exploration (2011-Present) – Co-designed and constructed novel buoyant under-ice rover for the traversal of arctic and Antarctic extreme environments. Primary operational use has been the investigation of methane seeps in permafrost lakes near Barrow, AK (Over 20 field campaigns completed, additional planned). Able to anchor in place for multiple days, provides video and instrument data. Rover is now remotely controllable via satellite from anywhere in the world for underwater operations. Responsible for electronics, programming, operations, and some mechanical construction. Also supporting long duration under-ice video/environmental sensor systems, and robotic kite-cams for aerial views.
  • JPL Engineering Lead - Ocean Exploration Vehicles (2016-Present) – Technical lead of novel systems to explore deep ocean and trenches in partnership with Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution. Includes Orpheus-class vehicles, Mini-Sub, other distributed architectures, and optical & sampling instrumentation.
  • Principal Investigator – INSPIRE: Interplanetary NanoSpacecraft Pathfinder In a Relevant Environment (2012-2014) – Led dual CubeSat mission to bring NanoSpacecraft to interplanetary space and demonstrate survivability, communications, navigation, COTS component monitoring, and novel science payloads. Team also developed micro-controller-based fault-tolerant software system (in conjunction with standard NASA MOS/GDS). Mission was selected for launch via NASA CLI - ready for flight, June 2014. Additional roles include Chief Engineer and MOS/GDS Lead. Funded by JPL and NASA Science Mission Directorate.
  • Mission Architect / System Engineer / Proposal Manager (2011-Present) – Support, provide analysis, and develop Earth and planetary mission concepts. Have led or supported proposals for ISS payloads, CubeSats, Small Spacecraft, Small-Body explorers, Interplanetary NanoSpacecraft, Jovian explorers / landers, Discovery & New Frontier missions, robotic-support for manned missions, etc. Provided analysis in system engineering, component selection, radiation analysis, orbital mechanics, and communication systems. Supported Europa Lander activities for sample handling mechanism and extremely low-risk planetary protection incendiary device.
  • CubeSat / NanoSpacecraft Consultant – Support design, construction and operations of NanoSpacecraft and CubeSats including M-Cubed/COVE (1&2), GRIFEX, CHARM/RACE, INSPIRE, RAX (1&2), ISARA, MarCO, NEAScout, Lunar Flashlight, ASTERIA, RainCube, AOSat, LunaH-Map, SunRISE

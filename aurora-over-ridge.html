Aurora Borealis Over the Northern Ridge

üìÖ 2024-01-20 üìç Coordinate: -77.840, 166.650

A Guardian in the Sky

Last night, the sky erupted in a dance of emerald and violet. The Aurora Borealis appeared shortly after midnight, casting a ghostly glow over the entire base camp.

It’s one thing to see photos, but standing in the biting cold and watching these ribbons of light shimmer across the sky is a humbling experience. The magnetic sensors in the lab were picking up significant activity, suggesting a major solar event.

We spent hours on the ridge with our long-exposure cameras, trying to capture the scale of the phenomenon. Even the base camp looked like a tiny outpost on a different planet under that light.

Events like this remind us why we endure the hardships of arctic research.
